The Map
Every dot is a real concert.
Each of the 56,000+ events is placed at its city's true geographic coordinates. Colour encodes genre: red for Rock, purple for EDM, orange for Hip-Hop, making the musical character of each city visible at a glance.
Click any dot to preview an event
The Vinyl
Each city is a record.
The vinyl markers are mini donut charts where arc length shows each genre's share of that city's total events. New York's cluster is the densest, Miami skews toward R&B and EDM, and Calgary leans heavily toward Country and Rock.

Seasonal Volume
Which cities host the most live music, and when? Monthly patterns across all 13 cities.
Volume
Summer dominates, though not everywhere.
Most cities peak in July–September. Miami is the outlier: its concert season peaks in winter (Jan–Mar) and nearly disappears in summer heat.
Click a heatmap cell to rank cities for that month
Scale
New York and LA dwarf the rest.
In raw counts, New York and LA each host roughly 10,000 events, about 4× Toronto and 13× Ottawa. Toggle to Per 100k population to see Detroit and Las Vegas punch above their weight.

Demand Mismatch
When do people want live music, and when does the industry actually deliver it? That gap reveals concrete programming opportunities.
Rhythm
Monday nights are the most underserved.
Across nearly every city, Monday evenings show the darkest red: Spotify listening demand is high, yet almost no live events are scheduled. For planners, that's a concrete and largely untapped gap.
Click any wedge to see demand vs. supply detail
Opportunity
Las Vegas is uniquely well-served on weekends.
Las Vegas is the only city where Saturday night is close to balanced. Everywhere else, Friday and Saturday still show moderate-to-high mismatch because audience demand consistently outpaces even the busiest nights.
Switch cities and months using the dropdowns
Why we built this
Cities are often described as "vibrant" or "culturally alive," but these impressions resist comparison. We wanted to make vitality concrete: not just how many events a city hosts, but when, where, and for whom the music actually plays.
