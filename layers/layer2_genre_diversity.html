A scatterplot is the clearest way to compare volume and diversity simultaneously. Using the average as the dividing line makes the four groups easier to read: above-average volume and diversity means a city is both busy and broad, while above-average volume but below-average diversity suggests scale without as much range.

We use music note icons instead of plain dots — the mark itself signals the domain, orienting the reader without sacrificing legibility. Note size stays fixed so position, not area, carries the data.

The note colour encodes the dominant Spotify listening genre (demand), while the treemap shows live event supply. A mismatch between the two is itself informative.

Encoding choices

Position (x, y) — most effective channel for bivariate quantitative comparison.

Colour hue — dominant genre as categorical nominal data.

Treemap area — part-to-whole genre breakdown on demand.
